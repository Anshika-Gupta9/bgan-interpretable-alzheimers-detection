The Problem

Why Alzheimer's detection is hard

Over 55 million people worldwide live with dementia. AI can help — but only if it reasons like a clinician, not a black box.

Progressive & irreversible

Alzheimer's causes 60–80% of all dementia cases. Structural MRI changes — hippocampal atrophy, ventricular enlargement — appear years before symptoms manifest, making early detection critical but difficult.

Black-box AI models

Existing CNNs achieve high accuracy but function as opaque systems. VGG16 reaches 94% accuracy with a Biomarker Alignment Score of 0.000 — it is right, but for the wrong reasons.

Interpretability gap

Post-hoc methods like GradCAM generate explanations after training with no guarantee they align with known neuroanatomy. Clinicians cannot trust predictions they cannot explain.

Project Overview

What makes BGAN different

BGAN is the only model in this study that is simultaneously competitive in accuracy and interpretable in its spatial attention behavior.

The core idea

Instead of learning attention purely from data, BGAN is guided by a Gaussian biomarker prior mask encoding nine clinically established neuroanatomical regions: bilateral hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, temporal lobes, lateral ventricles, and posterior parietal cortex.

A learnable scalar α blends data-driven and prior-guided attention, and a composite loss directly reinforces alignment during training.

The key metric: BAS

The Biomarker Alignment Score measures how much the model's attention concentrates on biomarker regions versus the rest:

BAS = mean(A* where M > 0.3) / mean(A* elsewhere)

BAS > 1 means the model focuses more on clinically relevant anatomy. BGAN achieves BAS = 11.585 ± 0.482.

1

DenseNet121 backbone

Pretrained on ImageNet. Dense skip connections promote feature reuse and efficient gradient flow, ideal for limited medical imaging data.

2

Channel Attention (SE-style)

Global average + max pooling → two FC layers → sigmoid weights each of the 1024 channels. Bottleneck ratio r=16. Emphasises discriminative feature maps.

3

Spatial Attention (CBAM-style)

Channel-wise avg and max pooling → Conv7×7 → sigmoid. Produces a [B, 1, 7, 7] map highlighting the most relevant spatial locations.

4

Gaussian biomarker prior mask

A soft 224×224 mask built from 9 weighted Gaussians centred on bilateral hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, temporal lobes, ventricles, and posterior parietal cortex.

5

Learnable blend α

A* = σ(α)·A_sp + (1−σ(α))·M_bio. Scalar α trained end-to-end, initialised to 0 (equal blend). Allows the model to learn how much to trust the prior.

6

Composite loss with λ-warmup

L = L_CE + λ(t)·L_bio. λ warms from 0.02 → 0.1 over 8 epochs. Prevents alignment loss from overwhelming classification signal early in training.

Dataset & Preprocessing

Rigorous, leak-free data handling

A pre-augmented Kaggle dataset with 44,000 MRI images. Standard random splitting would leak augmented twins across train/test — we fix this.

Stage 1 — MD5 deduplication

All 44,000 images byte-hashed. 462 exact byte-identical duplicates removed, yielding 43,538 unique images to start.

Stage 2 — perceptual hash clustering

pHash computed at 16×16. Pairs with Hamming distance ≤ 6 grouped using Union-Find. 29,023 near-duplicate pairs identified across 27,273 source groups.

Stage 3 — group-level stratified split

Splits at the group level, not image level. All augmented variants of a source scan stay together. Final: 32,341 train / 5,683 val / 5,514 test. Zero hash overlap verified.

Research Contributions

Novel contributions

Three independent contributions, each independently publishable, combining into a single cohesive framework.

BGAN Architecture

DenseNet121 augmented with SE-style channel attention and CBAM-style spatial attention, blended with a nine-region Gaussian biomarker prior via a learnable scalar α. Only 133K added parameters.

Composite loss + λ-warmup

L = L_CE + λ(t)·L_bio. The biomarker alignment term penalises attention outside biomarker regions. The λ warmup (0.02 → 0.1 over 8 epochs) prevents premature dominance of the alignment signal.

Biomarker Alignment Score

A novel, architecture-agnostic interpretability metric applicable to any model via GradCAM or native attention maps. BAS = mean(A in biomarker) / mean(A outside). Provides objective comparison of explanation quality.
